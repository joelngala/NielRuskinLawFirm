What To Do Immediately After An Arrest In Brooklyn

The first steps after an arrest can protect or damage your defense.

If you are arrested in Brooklyn, your first priority is not explaining yourself. It is protecting your rights and avoiding mistakes that prosecutors can later use against you.

1. Stay Silent

Give identifying information if required, but do not try to talk your way out of the arrest. People often assume they can clear things up. In practice, they usually give prosecutors extra statements to work with.

2. Ask For A Lawyer Clearly

Say that you want to speak with an attorney and then stop answering questions. Once you ask for counsel, keep your conduct consistent.

3. Understand The Arraignment Timeline

Arraignment typically happens quickly. That first appearance can determine release conditions, orders of protection, and the early narrative of the case.

Practical point: the earlier a defense lawyer gets involved, the earlier someone can start preserving facts, identifying witnesses, and checking whether the stop, search, or arrest was legally valid.

4. Do Not Discuss The Case By Phone Or Text

Calls from custody may be recorded. Messages to friends or family are often discoverable. Assume anything you write or say can be repeated in court.

5. Get A Real Assessment Early

The charge written on the paperwork is not always the charge that matters most later. Exposure, defenses, suppression issues, and settlement leverage depend on details that are not obvious to non-lawyers.
