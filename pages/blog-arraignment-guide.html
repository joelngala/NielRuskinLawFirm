What Happens At A Criminal Arraignment In New York?

A quick outline of the first court appearance and why it matters.

At arraignment, the court tells you the charges, addresses release conditions, and schedules the next step. It is not a minor administrative hearing. It often shapes the early leverage in the case.

Issues Commonly Addressed

  • The formal charge and accusatory instrument
  • Bail or release conditions
  • Orders of protection
  • Basic discovery scheduling
  • Whether the client leaves court or remains in custody

A defense lawyer at arraignment can immediately begin pushing back on overcharging, release restrictions, and one-sided versions of the facts before those assumptions harden.
