Practice Area

White-Collar Criminal Defense

Fraud, forgery, embezzlement, and document-heavy investigations require disciplined, strategic response.

These Cases Often Begin Before An Arrest

White-collar matters can start with subpoenas, target letters, or requests for interviews long before formal charges. Early defense can materially change the outcome.

Typical White-Collar Exposure

  • Fraud and scheme-to-defraud charges
  • Forgery and document allegations
  • Embezzlement and business disputes with criminal overlap
  • Financial records, electronic evidence, and intent issues

Neil Ruskin approaches these matters by controlling communications, narrowing factual disputes, and challenging whether the government can prove fraudulent intent rather than mere error or business conflict.
