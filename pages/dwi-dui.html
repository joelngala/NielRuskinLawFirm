Arrested for DWI in Brooklyn?

If you have been arrested for driving while intoxicated in Brooklyn or anywhere in New York City, you are facing serious consequences. A DWI conviction can mean license revocation, heavy fines, mandatory programs, and even jail time. The decisions you make in the days after an arrest can determine how your case turns out.

Neil Ruskin has defended hundreds of DWI and DWAI cases in Brooklyn criminal courts over more than three decades. As a former prosecutor, he knows how these cases are built and how to challenge them effectively.

Potential Penalties for DWI in New York

  • First offense DWI: Up to 1 year in jail, $500–$1,000 fine, license revocation for at least 6 months
  • Second offense DWI (within 10 years): Up to 4 years in prison, $1,000–$5,000 fine, license revocation for at least 1 year
  • Aggravated DWI (BAC 0.18+): Enhanced penalties, mandatory ignition interlock device
  • DWAI (Drugs): Up to 1 year in jail, $500–$1,000 fine, license revocation for at least 6 months

How Neil Ruskin Defends DWI Cases

Every DWI case has potential weaknesses. An experienced defense attorney will examine every detail, including:

  • The traffic stop itself. Was there a valid legal reason to pull you over? If the stop was unlawful, everything that follows may be inadmissible.
  • Field sobriety tests. These tests are subjective and frequently administered incorrectly. Weather, footwear, medical conditions, and road conditions can all affect results.
  • Breathalyzer accuracy. Breath testing machines require regular calibration and proper administration. Errors are common and can be challenged.
  • Blood test procedures. Chain of custody issues, contamination, and procedural errors can invalidate blood test results.
  • Miranda rights and questioning. If you were questioned without being read your rights after arrest, some statements may be suppressed.
  • Rising BAC defense. Your blood alcohol level at the time of testing may not reflect your level at the time of driving.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between DWI and DUI in New York?

In New York, DWI (Driving While Intoxicated) applies when a driver's blood alcohol content is 0.08% or higher. DWAI (Driving While Ability Impaired) applies at lower BAC levels or when impairment is caused by drugs. "DUI" is a general term used nationally but is not the formal charge in New York.

Can I lose my license for a first-time DWI in New York?

Yes. A first-time DWI conviction carries a mandatory license revocation of at least 6 months. However, an experienced defense attorney may be able to challenge the charges or negotiate a reduction to DWAI, which results in a 90-day suspension instead of revocation.

Should I refuse a breathalyzer test in New York?

Refusing a breathalyzer triggers an automatic license revocation of at least one year under New York's implied consent law, regardless of whether you are ultimately convicted. The refusal can also be used against you in court. Every situation is different, so consult an attorney immediately.

What happens at a DWI arraignment in Brooklyn?

At your arraignment, you will be formally charged and given the opportunity to enter a plea. The judge may set bail conditions or release you on your own recognizance. Having an attorney present at arraignment is critical because early decisions can significantly affect the trajectory of your case.
