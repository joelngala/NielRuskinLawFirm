Five Mistakes People Make After A DWI Arrest

The avoidable decisions that make impaired driving cases harder to defend.

  • Trying to explain the drinking timeline to police.
  • Assuming a first offense will be treated as minor.
  • Ignoring the license consequences separate from the criminal case.
  • Waiting too long to collect receipts, surveillance, or witness names.
  • Hiring based only on price instead of courtroom experience.

DWI cases often look simple from the outside, but the real fight usually involves the stop, the testing process, machine accuracy, timing, and whether the state can prove impairment the way it claims.

Early review matters because some defenses get weaker as time passes and evidence disappears.
