Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Criminal Defense

What should I do if I've been arrested in Brooklyn?

Do not speak to police without an attorney present. Exercise your right to remain silent and ask to speak with a lawyer immediately. Contact a criminal defense attorney as soon as possible, even before your arraignment. The earlier legal representation begins, the stronger your defense position.

How much does a criminal defense attorney cost?

Fees vary depending on the complexity and severity of the charges. Neil Ruskin offers a free initial consultation to discuss your case and provide a clear understanding of costs before you commit. The cost of not having experienced representation is almost always higher.

Can charges be dropped or reduced?

Yes, depending on the circumstances. Charges can be dismissed due to insufficient evidence, constitutional violations, procedural errors, or through negotiated plea agreements. An experienced defense attorney will evaluate every possible avenue for reduction or dismissal.

Do I really need a lawyer for a misdemeanor?

Yes. Even misdemeanor convictions can result in jail time, fines, probation, and a permanent criminal record that affects employment, housing, and immigration status. Having an experienced attorney can mean the difference between a conviction and a dismissal.

What is the difference between a misdemeanor and a felony in New York?

Misdemeanors carry potential sentences of up to one year in jail. Felonies carry potential state prison sentences of more than one year, with some charges carrying mandatory minimums. Both have serious long-term consequences for your record and future.
